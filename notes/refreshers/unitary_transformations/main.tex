\section{Quick refresher on unitary transformations}
\label{sec:unitary-transformations}

Let's say we have some Hamiltonian $\hat H$, which we examine in a basis $\{\ket{v_j}\}$ with
$j=1,\dots,\dim(\mathcal H)$ and $\hat H\colon\mathcal H\to\mathcal H$. The matrix
representation of the operator $\hat H$ in this basis is thus given by
\begin{equation}
H_{ij}=\braket{v_i}{\hat H\,v_j}=\bra{v_i}\hat H\ket{v_j}.
\end{equation}

Applying a unitary transformation $U$ (with $U^\dagger U=\mathds 1=UU^\dagger$), i.e.
\begin{equation}
\ket{v_j}\to\ket{\tilde v_j}=U\ket{v_j}\qquad\&\qquad\hat H\to\hat{\tilde H}=U\hat H U^\dagger,
\end{equation}
yields the matrix elements
\begin{align}
\tilde H_{ij}=\bra{\tilde v_i}\hat{\tilde H}\ket{\tilde v_j}
&=\bra{v_i}\underbrace{U^\dagger U}_{=\mathds 1}\hat H
\underbrace{U^\dagger U}_{=\mathds 1}\ket{v_j}
=\bra{v_i}\hat H\ket{v_j}=H_{ij}.
\end{align}
Hence the matrix representation of an operator does \emph{not} change under a unitary
transformation.
